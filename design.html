Visual Aesthetic

The visual aesthetic of my website is a harmonious fusion of sleek minimalist design and a light hearted cartoonish element. This blend results in a design that captures users attention through the cartoon images as well as providing an uncluttered and easily navigable website. The colour palette plays a pivotal role in achieving this aesthetic as the deep space blacks juxtaposed with white and a slight touch of yellow creates an overall visually aesthetic experience.

At the heart of the design are visuals that centred around the theme of space exploration in a cartoon style take. The homepage features a charming cartoon astronaut standing atop a yellow moon, planting a flag and extending a hand in a gesture. This whimsical portrayal represents the human desire for exploration and discovery. The astronaut's presence acts as a symbol of our innate curiosity, inviting users to explore my site and what it has to offer. The juxtaposition of the astronaut against the backdrop of space serves as a visual metaphor for the fusion of human ambition with the mysteries of space.

Imagery and Elements

Typography

The typography chosen for the website plays a pivotal role in reinforcing the sleek minimalist and space-themed design. "Orbitron" is selected as the font for headers and titles. Its sleek and modern appearance alludes to space-age technology and advancement, encapsulating the spirit of exploration. The careful spacing and sizing of fonts ensure a seamless reading experience allowing users to absorb the content.

The website's layout is thoughtfully structured to mirror the journey of cosmic exploration. The homepage has a welcome section, serving as a captivating entry point. Here, users are greeted by a statement that piques their curiosity. The introduction is enhanced by an animated orange "Explore" button that beckons users to initiate their voyage into my website. Additionally, the integration of NASA's "Astronomy Picture of the Day" presents users with daily images of celestial marvels and insights into them. Each blog post is meticulously framed against a cosmic backdrop, ensuring alignment with the overarching theme with each section being offset from the last with the same applying for the design page.

Layout and Composition

Hierarchy and Balance

The design's hierarchy is established through font selection and strategic placement of elements. Varying font sizes and boldness guide users' attention to pivotal content, allowing them to navigate with ease. The equilibrium is masterfully orchestrated, with elements thoughtfully distributed to create a symphony of visuals. The utilisation of white space contributes to a sense of balance, ensuring that the design remains uncluttered and user-friendly.

My overarching style goal is to envelop users in a journey of exploration throughout my website. The design's visual elements, colour palette, and subtle animations converge to create an experience that simultaneously educates and captivates. By harnessing minimalistic aesthetics and cosmic wonder, the design invites users to delve into the mysteries of space. The navigation bar at the top of the page is slightly transparent to add an additional layer of aesthetic and sleekness to the design of the pages.

Style and Aesthetic Goal

Selected Visual Design Elements

Aesthetic: The fusion of cosmic imagery, a simple colour palette, and sleek typography creates a sleek minimalistic atmosphere that resonates with the spirit of the vast void of space.
Dynamic Visuals: The incorporation of NASA's daily cosmic marvels ensures that the website's imagery is ever-evolving.

The carefully curated colour palette draws inspiration from the vast expanse of the cosmos:

#000000 - Used primarily in the background and for white space.
#fff - Primary colour for text throughout the website.
#f0bf55 - this yellow colour is used lightly across the site and can be seen in buttons and 'p' element dividers, bringing colour to the otherwise black and white website in a sleek and minimalist fashion.

These colours converge to create a harmonious visual tapestry that encapsulates the vast emptiness of space.

Graph Planning

Graph 1

1. Objective:
To provide users with a clear, visual representation of the size, proximity, and speed of near-Earth objects (NEOs) over a selected period, emphasizing potential threats based on size and distance from Earth.

2. Data Source:
NASA's Near Earth Object Web Service (NEOWS) - A freely available API offering detailed NEO information including size, velocity, and miss distance.

3. Features to Display:
Miss distance from Earth (X-axis)
Relative velocity (Y-axis)
Estimated diameter (represented by bubble size)

4. Visualization Tool:
D3.js - A JavaScript library for producing dynamic and interactive data visualizations in web browsers.

5. Graph Type:
Bubble chart.

6. Design:
A clean and minimalistic design with a dark background to emphasize the bubbles.
Axes labeled clearly with units.

1. Objective:
To visualize the solar flare activity over the past 12 days, showcasing the times of the day when these flares began.

2. Data Source:
NASA's DONKI API, specifically the FLR endpoint which provides details about solar flare activities.

3. Features to Display:
Date of flare activity (x-axis).
Time of the day when the flare began (y-axis).

4. Visualization Tool:
D3.js, a JavaScript library specifically for data-driven document transformations.

5. Graph Type:
Line graph to track the fluctuation of flare activity over time.

6. Design:
X-axis: Will represent days with clear date labels.
Y-axis: Will depict the time of the day, divided into 24 hours.
Path: A line connecting each data point, helping to trace the activity pattern.
Labels: Clear and concise, using the 'Orbitron' font-family for consistency with the website.
Colors: Line in orange for clarity against a dark background. Axis labels in white.

Graph 2

Graph 3

1. Objective:
To visualize the Coronal Mass Ejections (CMEs) over a user-defined period, showcasing the speed of these ejections and their start dates.

2. Data Source:
NASA's DONKI API, specifically the CME endpoint which provides details about Coronal Mass Ejections

3. Features to Display:
Start date of the CME activity (x-axis).
Speed of the CME in kilometers per second (y-axis).
Interactivity: A slider allowing users to adjust the time frame, ranging from 7 to 30 days, to view CMEs within that period.

4. Visualization Tool:
D3.js - A JavaScript library for producing dynamic and interactive data visualizations in web browsers.

5. Graph Type:
Scatter plot with connected lines to represent the progression of CME speeds over the selected time frame.

6. Design:
X-axis: Represents the start dates of CMEs with clear date labels
Y-axis: Depicts the speed of the CMEs in kilometers per second.
Dots: Represent individual CME events, with their position determined by the start date and speed.
Lines: Connect the dots in chronological order, providing a visual representation of the trend in CME speeds.
Slider: Positioned below the graph, allowing users to adjust the time frame of displayed data.
Labels: Clear and concise, using the 'Orbitron' font-family for consistency with the website and to maintain a futuristic aesthetic.
Colors: Dots and lines in orange for visibility against a dark background. Axis labels in white for contrast. The slider in orange to maintain consistency with the graph's color scheme.
